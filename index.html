Load lung-field screenshots. The tool auto-detects large airways and excludes a small ring around them (peribronchial walls are anatomically thicker and confound percent-collagen). The Ashcroft estimate is driven by wall thickness and fibrotic-mass area, not just collagen percent — so it works even when the stain reads pink/purple rather than classic blue.

Airway detection erodes the airspace mask 2 px first (so alveoli joined by thin wall gaps don't merge into one giant "airway"), then flags eroded components ≥ N× median alveolus AND ≤ 15% of ROI. The peri-airway buffer is excluded so peribronchial wall thickening cannot inflate the score.

Ashcroft scoring reference (0–8)
GradeHistological criteria
0Normal lung.
1Minimal fibrous thickening of alveolar or bronchiolar walls.
2Moderate thickening of walls without obvious damage to lung architecture.
3Increased fibrosis with definite damage to lung structure and formation of fibrous bands or small fibrous masses.
4Severe distortion of structure and large fibrous areas; "honeycomb lung" placed in this category.
5Confluent fibrous masses with definite damage; some preserved airspaces remain.
6Large contiguous fibrous masses; only occasional airspaces, largely filled with cells/debris.
7Severe distortion; airspaces nearly obliterated, occasional air bubbles only.
8Total fibrous obliteration of the field.

Modified Ashcroft scale (Ashcroft et al. 1988; Hübner et al. 2008). Score the worst-affected representative high-power field; assign the highest grade present in >~1 field.

Fibrotic-mass detector

Stain matrices: trichrome — collagen [0.799, 0.591, 0.105], cytoplasm [0.099, 0.737, 0.668], nuclei [0.650, 0.704, 0.286]. H&E — eosin [0.072, 0.990, 0.105], nuclei [0.644, 0.717, 0.267]. Airway detection erodes the airspace mask 2 px first; flagged components ≥ N× median alveolus and ≤ 15% ROI become airways, then the peri-airway buffer extends the exclusion. Vessel detection finds round tissue islands ≥ 0.6 circularity surrounded by collagen/RBCs.

Ashcroft scoring reference (0–8)
GradeHistological criteria
0Normal lung.
1Minimal fibrous thickening of alveolar or bronchiolar walls.
2Moderate thickening of walls without obvious damage to lung architecture.
3Increased fibrosis with definite damage to lung structure and formation of fibrous bands or small fibrous masses.
4Severe distortion of structure and large fibrous areas; "honeycomb lung" placed in this category.
5Confluent fibrous masses with definite damage; some preserved airspaces remain.
6Large contiguous fibrous masses; only occasional airspaces, largely filled with cells/debris.
7Severe distortion; airspaces nearly obliterated, occasional air bubbles only.
8Total fibrous obliteration of the field.

Modified Ashcroft scale (Ashcroft et al. 1988; Hübner et al. 2008). Score the worst-affected representative high-power field.

Prototype. All processing is local — closing the tab clears every sheet, so export before you leave. Each loaded image holds its full bitmap in memory; for very large sets work one mouse per sheet and export as you go. The RT 81-3 reference set ships with the tool: when you load files named 1.tif–37.tif the validation panel automatically scores the tool against the 3-rater consensus.
